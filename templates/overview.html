Overview


The Breast Cancer Risk Navigation (BNAV) tool is a web-based decision support tool with modules that present pertinent information for primary care providers regarding breast cancer risk assessment and preventative measures for their patients.


Increasing Breast Cancer Chemoprevention in the Primary Care Setting
(NIH/NCI R01 CA177995-01A1)

The purpose of this randomized controlled trial is to evaluate a web-based patient-centered decision aid (RealRisks) designed to inform patients about breast cancer risk and chemoprevention with estrogen-blocking drugs, such as tamoxifen. It is coupled with a physician-centered decision support tool, BNAV, to enhance shared decision-making about chemoprevention in the primary care setting. The investigators hypothesize that improving accuracy of breast cancer risk perceptions and understanding of the risks and benefits of breast cancer chemoprevention will increase the uptake and informed choice about chemoprevention among high-risk women in the primary care setting.

Decision Support for BRCA Testing in Ethnically Diverse Women
(American Cancer Society, RSG-17-103-01)

The objective of this research study is to expand BRCA genetic testing for women who are at increased risk of breast and ovarian cancer due to their family history of cancer. We propose to promote referrals to genetic counseling by primary care providers (PCPs) and appropriate genetic testing for hereditary breast and ovarian cancer (HBOC). We intend to increase referrals in primary care clinics by using a patient-centered decision aid called RealRisks and a provider-centered BNAV decision support tool, along with an electronic health record (EHR)-embedded Ambulatory Medicine dashboard. We hypothesize that combining a patient-centered decision aid with a provider-centered decision support tool integrated into clinic workflow will increase appropriate uptake of genetic counseling among racially and ethnically diverse women.
